Bill of Rights

The Bill of Rights is a set of protections that was used by the Anti-Federalists during the ratification debates. The Bill of Rights was a stipulation put in place in order to secure more protections from the potential Tyranny of the Majority they were so afraid of.

Required Court Cases Overview

Case Amendment/Concept Description Outcome
Engel V. Vital (1962) 1st Amendment; Establishment Clause Incorporated the 1st Amendment to the states by asking if public school prayer was constitutional. The state cannot organize prayer in public school, even if the prayer is non-denominational and students are given options for non-participation.
Wisconsin v. Yoder (1972) 1st Amendment; Free Exercise Clause Yoder was an Amish person and, under the Free Exercise Clause, argued that the Wisconsin Compulsory Attendance Law was infringing on his rights. Incorporated the Free Exercise Clause to the states. The Supreme Court ruled that the free exercise clause of the First Amendment, as incorporated by the Fourteenth Amendment, prevented the state of Wisconsin from compelling the Amish to send their children to formal secondary school beyond the age of fourteen.
McDonald v. Chicago 2nd Amendment Otis McDonald and others were banned from personally owning firearms for self-defense because of Chicago's firearms laws. Amendment now applies to state and local government through the doctrine of selective incorporation.
